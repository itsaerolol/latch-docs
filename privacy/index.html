Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 15, 2026

Latch is a Screen Time app that helps you shield chosen apps behind a short, deliberate friction gate. This policy explains what the app does — and, mostly, does not do — with your information.

The short version

Latch has no user accounts, collects no analytics, and runs no servers. Everything the app knows about you — your sessions, stats, streaks, app-group selections, and settings — is stored only on your device, inside a private container shared between the app and its widgets. Nothing you do in Latch — starting a latch, your stats, your settings — is transmitted to us or to any third party. The only network activity is Apple's own handling of purchases, which never passes through us.

No accounts

There is no sign-up, no sign-in, and no personal profile. You never give Latch a name, an email address, or any other identifying information beyond an optional display name you can type into Settings, which is used only to personalize the app's own greeting text and is never sent off your device.

No analytics, no telemetry, no third-party SDKs

Latch does not use any analytics, crash-reporting, or advertising SDKs. We do not know how often you open the app, which features you use, or anything else about your behavior. We do not collect this information or transmit it anywhere.

No servers

Latch has no backend. All data — latch session history, streaks, weekly-goal progress, app-group definitions, and your settings — is written to an on-device App Group container (a private storage area shared only between the Latch app and its own widget and system extensions) using Apple's standard UserDefaults storage. No session, stats, streak, app-group, or settings data ever leaves your device over the network. The one exception is purchases, which are processed entirely by Apple — see "Subscriptions and purchases" below.

Family Controls / Screen Time

Latch uses Apple's Family Controls and Screen Time frameworks to let you choose which apps and categories get shielded during a latch, and to enforce that shield. When you pick apps using Apple's system picker, Latch receives only an opaque token representing your selection. It stores that token on your device so your selection persists between sessions, but it cannot decode the token into a list of which apps you picked, their names, or your usage of them — that remains reserved to iOS and is never available to Latch. This is enforced by Apple's operating system, not by a promise we're making on top of it.

Notifications

Latch schedules local, on-device notifications only — for example, to tell you when a latch session has completed. These are not push notifications and do not involve any notification server operated by us or anyone else.

Subscriptions and purchases

Latch Pro is offered as an auto-renewing monthly or yearly subscription, or a one-time lifetime purchase, through Apple's App Store. All payment processing, billing information, and receipts are handled entirely by Apple under Apple's own privacy policy — Latch's own code never receives, stores, or transmits your payment details. Latch only ever learns whether you currently hold an active entitlement (Pro or not, and which plan), not any billing information behind it. The yearly plan may include a free trial period; the monthly plan does not.

Data retention and deletion

Because everything is stored only on your device, deleting the Latch app deletes all of its data along with it — there is no copy anywhere else to separately request or wait on. Within the app, Settings also offers ways to reset specific pieces of data (like your golden-ticket count or weekly-goal progress) without deleting the whole app.

Children's privacy

Because Latch collects no personal data from anyone, of any age, there is nothing age-specific to disclose beyond what's already described above. Latch is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect information from them — mechanically, it can't, since it doesn't collect information from anyone.

Your rights under GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, and similar laws

Latch collects no personal data, runs no servers, and does not sell or share any data. Because there is no data held about you anywhere outside your own device, there is nothing for us to access, export, correct, or delete in response to a rights request — deleting the app removes everything, as described above. For the limited on-device processing Latch performs, Latch's developer (Keaton White) is the data controller. If you are in the EU, UK, California, or a comparable jurisdiction and have questions, contact us at the address below.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the effective date above and, for any change that meaningfully affects how the app handles your data, call it out in the app's release notes.
